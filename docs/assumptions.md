# Assumptions

This document outlines the key assumptions used in the Net Zero 2030 – Carbon Strategy Dashboard project.

## 1. Data Assumptions

The project uses sample/synthetic data created for portfolio purposes.

No real company, client, or confidential data is used.

The data is designed to simulate a company with emissions across Scope 1, Scope 2, and Scope 3.

---

## 2. Baseline Year

The baseline year is assumed to be 2023.

All emissions reduction scenarios are compared against this baseline year.

---

## 3. Emissions Units

Emissions are expressed in tonnes of carbon dioxide equivalent.

Unit used:

tCO₂e

---

## 4. Scope 1 Assumptions

Scope 1 emissions are assumed to come from direct operational sources.

Examples include:

- Fuel use
- Company-owned vehicles
- On-site combustion

---

## 5. Scope 2 Assumptions

Scope 2 emissions are assumed to come from purchased electricity.

The project assumes that electricity-related emissions are calculated using an emission factor applied to electricity consumption.

---

## 6. Scope 3 Assumptions

Scope 3 emissions are assumed to represent the largest share of total emissions.

This reflects a common situation in many companies where value chain emissions are larger than direct operational emissions.

Scope 3 categories may include:

- Purchased goods and services
- Supplier emissions
- Logistics
- Business travel
- Waste
- Other upstream or downstream activities

---

## 7. Emission Factors

Emission factors are sample values used to demonstrate the emissions calculation workflow.

They are not intended to represent official or location-specific emission factors.

In a real project, emission factors should be sourced from recognized databases or official methodologies.

---

## 8. Carbon Pricing

Carbon pricing values are simulated to estimate potential financial exposure from emissions.

The carbon price is used to calculate potential carbon cost and long-term climate-related financial risk.

---

## 9. Abatement Measures

Abatement measures are simulated examples of decarbonization initiatives.

They are used to estimate:

- Emissions reduction potential
- Implementation cost
- Annual financial benefit
- ROI
- Payback period

---

## 10. Financial Metrics

ROI and payback calculations are simplified for portfolio demonstration purposes.

They are intended to show how sustainability initiatives can be evaluated using business and financial logic.

---

## 11. Dashboard Assumptions

Dashboard tables are prepared to support Power BI visualizations.

The dashboard is designed for executive-level decision-making and ESG reporting support.

---

## 12. Project Limitation

This project is not intended to provide real emissions reporting or verified carbon accounting.

It is a portfolio project created to demonstrate:

- ESG data structuring
- Carbon accounting logic
- SQL database design
- Power BI dashboard preparation
- Decarbonization analysis
- Sustainability reporting communication
